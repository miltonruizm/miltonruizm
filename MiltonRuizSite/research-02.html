Binary Neutron Star Mergers

Although the aLIGO/Virgo collaboration has reported detections of a few neutron star-black holes (NSBH) binaries candidates it is not known yet that such systems can be the progenitors of central engines that power sGRBs. I have demonstrated, for the first time in full GR, that if the initial spin of the BH is larger than 0.5 and the mass ratio MNS/MBH = 3 then NSBH systems can launch a magnetically-driven jet. The lifetime of the jets and their outgoing Poynting luminosities [Ljet ∼ 1052 erg/s] are consistent with typical sGRBs, as well as with the Blandford–Znajek mechanism for launching jets and their associated Poynting luminosities.
